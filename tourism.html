Udayagiri

The caves were constructed in the first century by King Kharavela of Kalinga, ancient Odisha (approximately 209-after 170 BC). The great Mauryan King Ashoka conquered Kharavela. Kharavela was a liberal when it came to religious matters, notwithstanding his Jainism. Between the 7th and 12th centuries, Udayagiri, also known as Madhavapura Mahavihara, was a major Buddhist center. Udayagiri's excavations partially uncovered a double-story monastic complex dating from the eighth century A.D. Hathi Gumpha, Ananta Gumpha, Ganesha Gumpha, Jaya Vijaya Gumpha, Mancapuri Gumpha, Bagha/Vyaghra/Vyaghra Gumpha, and Sarpa Gumpha are all famous caves.

Deomali

Deomali Peak, with an elevation of about 1,672 m, is the highest peak in the state of Odisha. It is situated near Barabandha village, Kotia Panchayat division which is about 70 km from Koraput via Semiliguda. Surrounded by deep green forest, the peak is rich in flora and fauna. This hill range is rich in mineral resources such as bauxite, limestone and gemstones. Deomali is dotted with brooks and deep valleys, and inhabited by tribes such as Kandhas, Parajas, Bhumia, Malis and Bhotias. It is not only a marvel for the tourists but also to the adventurous sports lovers for hang gliding, mountaineering and trekking. In recent days, the Odisha tourism Department, has been taking many development steps to popularize this peak as a hot tourist spot by connecting the Tar roads, Hill top amenities centre, drinking water points on the hill top, and watching towers for good views of the arena. Many waterfalls are also found while heading for the top of hill.
